Bacteria

Campylobacter

Incubation period

2-5 days (range 1-11 days)

Description

Of the eight species of Campylobacter considered to be of clinical importance, C. jejuni is responsible for the majority of human infections.

Symptoms

Fever, severe abdominal pain, nausea and diarrhoea which can vary from slight to profuse and watery.

Mode of transmission

Ingestion of contaminated food. Main food sources are raw milk and raw or undercooked poultry. Spread to other foods by cross-contamination or contamination with untreated water.
